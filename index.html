about

I’ve always been interested in learning new skills and building things from scratch, meaning I’m most happy when I’m making a piece of jewellery for a friend, upcycling a tired piece of furniture or, if I’m really lucky, putting together an item from Ikea. It’s that creative satisfaction that comes with completing hands-on projects that e ncouraged me to research a career in web development.

While working in the publishing industry, I started to notice a few women around me who were learning to code and it piqued an interest in me, so much so that I started to look into what ‘coding’ actually entailed. I was drawn to the problem-solving aspect of web development and the fact that it was a skill I could learn, hone and be proud of. I decided to find a role in a tech company to assimile myself with this new industry and find out a bit more about the world of programming.

I took the plunge and joined the General Assembly Software Engineering Immersive course which was a hugely rewarding challenge. With all of the new technical skills I’ve gained throughout the course, I’m excited to finally be able to realise the multitude of project ideas that have started to fill up my iPhone notes. On top of these skills, I learnt an enormous amount about myself and what I’m capable of achieving in such a short period of time. I’m excited to take these new skills into a web development role so that I can continue this new life-long career of coding and learning.
